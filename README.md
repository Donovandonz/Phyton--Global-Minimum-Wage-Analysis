# Global Minimum Wage Analysis: Detailed Insights

## Executive Summary

Analysis of 186 countries reveals three distinct economic worlds in global labor markets, with an 8,458-fold gap between highest and lowest minimum wages.

## Key Statistical Findings

### Global Distribution
- **Mean**: $468.46 (skewed by outliers)
- **Median**: $280.00 (true central tendency)
- **Standard Deviation**: $544.75 (extreme variability)
- **Coefficient of Variation**: 1.16 (very high dispersion)

### Regional Analysis

#### Europe: The High-Wage Leader
- **Average**: $996 (113% above global)
- **Top**: Luxembourg ($2,876)
- **Bottom**: Still $193 (higher than 80% of world)
- **Insight**: Economic integration and strong labor protections

#### Africa: Development Challenge
- **Average**: $151 (68% below global)
- **8 of bottom 10 countries** are African
- **Burundi**: $0.34/month
- **Insight**: Informal economies and political instability

#### The Caribbean Exception
- **Saint Kitts and Nevis**: #7 globally ($1,999)
- **Grenada**: #8 globally ($1,969)
- **Why**: Tourism-driven economies, small populations

## Critical Thresholds

| Threshold | Countries | % of Total | Economic Implication |
|-----------|-----------|------------|---------------------|
| >$1,000 | 19 | 10.2% | Developed economies |
| $500-$1,000 | 28 | 15.1% | Emerging markets |
| $250-$500 | 37 | 19.9% | Developing nations |
| $100-$250 | 36 | 19.4% | Low-income countries |
| <$100 | 66 | 35.5% | Extreme poverty |

## The Three Worlds Framework

### World 1: High-Income (19 countries)
- **Characteristics**: Western Europe, Caribbean outliers
- **Average wage**: >$1,500
- **Key features**: Strong institutions, formal economies

### World 2: Middle-Income (65 countries)
- **Characteristics**: Latin America, parts of Asia
- **Average wage**: $250-$1,000
- **Key features**: Mixed economies, growing formal sector

### World 3: Low-Income (102 countries)
- **Characteristics**: Sub-Saharan Africa, parts of Asia
- **Average wage**: <$250
- **Key features**: Informal economies, development challenges

## Policy Recommendations

### For International Organizations
1. Create targeted programs for the 66 countries under $100/month
2. Study Caribbean success stories for small-economy models
3. Develop metrics beyond minimum wage for informal economies

### For National Governments
1. **High-income countries**: Maintain social protections
2. **Middle-income**: Gradual increases with productivity
3. **Low-income**: Formal economy expansion first

### For Researchers
1. Investigate Caribbean economic models
2. Study correlation between minimum wage and informal sector
3. Analyze successful transitions across the $100 threshold

## Data Quality Notes
- **Coverage**: 186 countries (2024-2025)
- **Standardization**: All wages converted to monthly USD
- **Limitations**: Exchange rate fluctuations, informal economies not captured
